    Under the Hood

    How It Works

    We go beyond simple rule checks — our engine calculates information entropy to give you a true measure of password strength.

    🧮

    Entropy Calculation

    Entropy measures the randomness of your password in bits. A password with N possible characters and length L has entropy E = L × log₂(N). More bits = exponentially harder to crack.

    ⏳

    Crack-Time Estimation

    We model offline attacks at 10 billion guesses/second (modern GPU clusters). The time to brute-force is 2^E / guesses_per_sec, giving you a realistic threat picture.

    📋

    Common Password Detection

    Your password is checked against a curated list of the most commonly breached passwords. If it matches, it can be cracked instantly via dictionary attack — regardless of its entropy.

    🧠

    Pattern Analysis

    We detect predictable patterns like sequential characters (abc, 123), repeated characters, and keyboard walks (qwerty), which significantly reduce effective entropy.

    Leaked passwords from one breach are used to unlock accounts on other services.

    Best Practices

    Tips for Strong Passwords

    Follow these expert-recommended strategies to keep your accounts secure.

    01

    Use a Password Manager

    Let a password manager generate and store unique, complex passwords for every account. You only need to remember one master password.

    02

    Go Long, Not Complex

    A 20-character passphrase like correct-horse-battery-staple is stronger — and easier to remember — than P@$$w0rd!.

    03

    Enable Two-Factor Auth

    Even the strongest password can be phished. Add a second factor (TOTP, hardware key) so a compromised password alone isn't enough.

    04

    Never Reuse Passwords

    One breach exposes every account that shares the same password. Unique passwords contain the blast radius of any single leak.

    05

    Avoid Personal Info

    Names, birthdays, and pet names are easily guessable through social engineering. Keep your passwords disconnected from your identity.

    06

    Audit Regularly

    Check haveibeenpwned.com periodically. If any of your credentials appear in a breach, change them immediately.
